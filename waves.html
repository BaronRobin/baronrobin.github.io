Project Details

Wavy Cubes

In this project we had to create a system with exactly 34 by 21 cubes. Then we had to change the apperance by adding some modifiers in 3ds Max.

Personally, I found it pretty fun because we could explore the programm in a new more playful way. There was no moddeling or programming. Just add some modifiers and play around with the values, change the order of them and see what changes.

I could also play around the materials and came up with some interesting combinations. I remember opening the extendet material editior and being overwhelmed of it, now I love it, because I only knew the blender Shader Editor and looking back at it, it feels pretty cluttered.

Behind the scenes - Inside of 3ds Max

Short disclaimer

All visuals above were rendered in 3ds Max with the Arnold render engine. After the first semester, I got myself the VRay License and since then are using it to create my renderings.

It also was a fun way to play around with the lights and exploring how different angles give different results. All in all, I would say it was my favorite task in this subject in the first semester.

Used Modifiers

  • Bend 90°, Direction 0.
  • Wave amp1 3,48m.
  • Wave amp2 -4,14m.
  • Wave len 27m.
  • Wave Phase 0,0 - Decay 0,0.
